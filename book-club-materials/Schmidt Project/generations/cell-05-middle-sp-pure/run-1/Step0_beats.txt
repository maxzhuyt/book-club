# Key Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of "The Impeachment of Abraham Lincoln"

Based on the novel's premise and opening, here are the 5 most important structural elements the first chapter needs to establish:

1. **Establish the Alternate History Hook** - The chapter must immediately orient readers to the "what if" premise: Lincoln survived the assassination attempt at Ford's Theatre. This counterfactual foundation is essential for everything that follows.

2. **Introduce the Protagonist** - Establish Abigail Canner as the central character—a young Black woman in post-Civil War Washington D.C. who has just graduated from law school. Her unique perspective as both an outsider and insider to the legal/political world should be clear.

3. **Set the Historical/Political Stakes** - Ground the reader in the political climate of 1867 Washington, where Lincoln faces impeachment proceedings. The tension between Reconstruction-era politics and the looming trial creates the central conflict.

4. **Establish Tone and Atmosphere** - Blend the legal thriller genre with historical fiction, creating a sense of both period authenticity and narrative suspense that will carry through the novel.

5. **Create Narrative Momentum** - End with a hook or development that pulls readers into the larger mystery/thriller plot, connecting Abigail to the impeachment proceedings in a way that makes her involvement essential.
