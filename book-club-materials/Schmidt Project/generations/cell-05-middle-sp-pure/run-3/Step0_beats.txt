# Key Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of "The Impeachment of Abraham Lincoln"

Based on the novel's premise and opening, here are the five most important structural elements the first chapter needs to accomplish:

1. **Establish the Alternate History Premise** - The chapter must quickly orient readers to the central "what if" scenario: Lincoln survived the assassination attempt at Ford's Theatre and now faces impeachment in 1867. This foundational worldbuilding beat grounds everything that follows.

2. **Introduce the Protagonist** - Abigail Canner, a young Black woman who has just graduated from Oberlin College, needs to be established with her key characteristics: her intelligence, ambition, and the unique challenges she faces as a woman of color in post-Civil War Washington.

3. **Set Up the Central Conflict/Mystery** - The chapter should introduce the tensions surrounding Lincoln's impeachment and hint at the deeper intrigue that will drive the plot, creating narrative questions that pull readers forward.

4. **Establish Time, Place, and Atmosphere** - Ground readers in 1867 Washington D.C., capturing the political climate, social tensions of Reconstruction, and the historical texture of the era.

5. **Create Stakes and Forward Momentum** - Give Abigail a concrete goal or opportunity (her position at a law firm involved in Lincoln's defense) that launches her into the main narrative and establishes what she stands to gain or lose.
