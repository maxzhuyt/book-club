# Key Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of "The Impeachment of Abraham Lincoln"

Based on the novel's premise and opening, here are the 5 most important structural elements a first chapter in this alternate history legal thriller would need to establish:

1. **Establish the Alternate History Premise** - The chapter must quickly orient readers to the "what if" scenario: Lincoln survived the assassination attempt, the Civil War ended, and now (in 1867) he faces impeachment proceedings. This world-building foundation is essential.

2. **Introduce the Protagonist** - Abigail Canner, a young Black woman who has just graduated from law school, needs to be established with her distinctive voice, ambitions, and the challenges she faces in this era as both a woman and a person of color entering the legal profession.

3. **Set Up the Central Conflict/Mystery** - Plant the seeds of the legal and political intrigue surrounding Lincoln's impeachment that will drive the plot forward, giving readers a sense of the stakes involved.

4. **Establish Tone and Atmosphere** - Blend the historical setting of Reconstruction-era Washington D.C. with the tension of a legal/political thriller, creating the novel's distinctive atmosphere.

5. **Create a Hook/Inciting Incident** - Provide a compelling reason for readers to continue, likely connecting Abigail to the impeachment case in some meaningful way that launches the narrative.
