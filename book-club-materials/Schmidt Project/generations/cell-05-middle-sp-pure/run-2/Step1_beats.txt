# Key Structural Beats for Original Scene: Winter Palace Memo, 1885

For a scene depicting a bureaucrat drafting an agrarian reform memo in this alternate history where Alexander II survived:

1. **Establish the Bureaucrat's Position and Stakes** - Introduce the character's rank within the imperial administration, their relationship to the reform program, and what personal or professional consequences hinge on this memo's reception. Are they a true believer in reform, a cautious careerist, or a reluctant participant navigating between factions?

2. **Ground the Alternate Timeline Through Concrete Details** - Weave in specific references to how Russia has changed since 1881 without the assassination. Perhaps mention continued emancipation policies, Alexander's aging but persistent presence, or the frustrated reactionaries waiting in the wings. The memo itself becomes a vehicle for this exposition.

3. **Create Tension Through the Writing Process** - The act of drafting should reveal internal conflict. What does the bureaucrat choose to include or omit? Where does the pen hesitate? What phrases get crossed out and rewritten? This transforms a static administrative task into dramatic action.

4. **Introduce External Pressure or Interruption** - Break the solitary drafting with an intrusion: a colleague's visit, overheard conversation, a summons, or disturbing news. This creates movement and hints at larger political forces surrounding the document.

5. **End with Uncertainty or Complication** - Close the scene with the memo's fate unresolved or with new information that changes its significance, propelling the narrative forward.
