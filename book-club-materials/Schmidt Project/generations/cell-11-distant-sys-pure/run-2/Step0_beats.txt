# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *Roma Eterna* by Robert Silverberg

Based on the novel's opening chapter "With Caesar in the Underworld" (set in A.U.C. 1282/529 CE in this alternate timeline):

1. **Establish the Alternate World** - Introduce the reader to a Roman Empire that never fell, conveying through details and atmosphere that this is a divergent history where Rome remained the dominant world power.

2. **Introduce the Protagonist's Status Quo** - Present the main character (typically a Roman citizen of some standing) in their ordinary world, establishing their role, desires, and relationship to Imperial society.

3. **Create Social/Political Tension** - Hint at the intrigue, corruption, or instability within the eternal empire, suggesting that immortality as a civilization comes with its own decadence and complications.

4. **Inciting Incident/Disruption** - Introduce an event, discovery, or summons that pulls the protagonist out of routine and sets the chapter's central conflict in motion.

5. **Hook for the Larger Narrative** - End with a development that raises questions about Rome's continued dominance or the protagonist's fate, connecting this self-contained episode to the novel's broader mosaic structure.
