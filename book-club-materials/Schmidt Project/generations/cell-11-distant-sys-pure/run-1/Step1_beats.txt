# Structural Beats for Treasury Scene - Baghdad, 1100 CE

**Premise:** The Abbasid Caliphate remains unified; al-Andalus is still an integrated province rather than independent successor states.

---

1. **Open with Bureaucratic Ritual as Worldbuilding** - Begin mid-task as the official handles physical documents (scrolls, ledgers, seals), using the mundane mechanics of imperial taxation to demonstrate the administrative apparatus that holds this vast polity together across thousands of miles.

2. **Reveal Distance and Unity Through Discrepancy** - Introduce a specific irregularity or notable detail in the Andalusian returns (unusual commodity, adjusted rate, delayed shipment) that forces the official to contemplate the western province, creating natural exposition about how center and periphery remain connected.

3. **Embed the Counterfactual Through Casual Reference** - Have the official mentally compare al-Andalus to other provinces or recall historical precedent, referencing events or institutions that exist only because fragmentation never occurred—treated as unremarkable background knowledge rather than explained history.

4. **Introduce Human Stakes Beneath the Numbers** - Ground the abstraction of taxation in specific consequence—a name in the ledger, a petitioner waiting outside, a governor's reputation—transforming bureaucratic procedure into something with weight and implication for real lives.

5. **Close with Unresolved Administrative Tension** - End not with dramatic climax but with a decision deferred, a document set aside, or a question forwarded upward—suggesting the grinding continuity of imperial governance while creating subtle forward momentum.
