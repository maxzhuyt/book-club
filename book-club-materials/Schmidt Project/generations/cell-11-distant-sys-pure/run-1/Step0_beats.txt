# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *Roma Eterna* by Robert Silverberg

Based on the opening chapter "With Caesar in the Underworld" (set in A.U.C. 1282/529 CE in this alternate timeline):

1. **Establish the Alternate World Premise** - Immediately ground the reader in this divergent history where Rome never fell, introducing the continuing Roman Empire through sensory details, terminology, and cultural markers that feel both familiar and strange.

2. **Introduce the Protagonist's Status Quo** - Present the main character (typically a Roman citizen navigating imperial society) in their ordinary world, establishing their role, desires, and relationship to power structures within the eternal empire.

3. **Create Tension Through Political/Social Intrigue** - Introduce an inciting conflict or mission that pulls the protagonist into larger imperial machinations, reflecting the Byzantine complexity of a Rome that has endured centuries longer.

4. **Demonstrate the "What If" Through Concrete Absence** - Show what does NOT exist in this world (Christianity's triumph, the fall of classical civilization) through what IS present, making the alternate history tangible rather than abstract.

5. **End with Forward Momentum** - Close with a hook that propels the narrative forward while raising questions about both the immediate plot and the larger nature of this eternal Roman civilization.

---
*Note: Roma Eterna is a fix-up novel; individual chapters function as linked novellas with distinct structures.*
