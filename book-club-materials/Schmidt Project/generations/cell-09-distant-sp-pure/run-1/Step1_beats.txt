# Structural Beats for Original Scene: The Dispatches at Lin'an

**Premise:** A Song minister receives news of the Mongol failure at Xiangyang, autumn 1273.

1. **Open with Atmospheric Unease** — Establish the minister in his study or office amid the late autumn setting, carrying the weight of months of dread; the capital has lived under the shadow of Xiangyang's potential fall, and this tension should permeate the physical space before any news arrives.

2. **The Reading as Revelation** — Structure the dispatch reading as a layered unfolding—initial disbelief, verification of details, the specific tactical information about how the defenses held—allowing the minister (and reader) to absorb the magnitude gradually rather than all at once.

3. **Complicate the Relief with Political Calculus** — Even in this moment of deliverance, introduce the minister's awareness of what comes next: which generals will claim credit, which factions will leverage this survival, what the emperor's response will demand of the court.

4. **Personal Stakes Beneath Official Duty** — Reveal through brief reflection someone the minister knew at Xiangyang—a cousin, former student, or old friend—grounding the historical event in intimate human connection and the particular grief or relief that accompanies survival.

5. **End on Uncertain Horizon** — Close with the minister's gaze turning outward (literally or metaphorically), acknowledging that survival is not victory; the Mongols remain, winter approaches, and Kitai's fate remains unwritten.
