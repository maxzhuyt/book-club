# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *River of Stars*

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Historical-Mythic Frame** — Open with the narrative voice that situates the reader in a civilization past its golden age (the fallen Twelfth Dynasty/Tang analogue), creating elegiac distance and thematic weight about the passage of glory.

2. **Introduce Lin Shan Through Defining Action** — Present the female protagonist in a scene that demonstrates her exceptional education, wit, and the tension between her talents and the constraints placed on women in Kitai society.

3. **Ground the Political Context** — Weave in essential exposition about the current emperor, the lost northern territories (the Fourteen Prefectures), and the court's complicated relationship with the steppe peoples—establishing the novel's central historical wound.

4. **Set the Tonal Duality** — Balance intimate, personal moments with sweeping historical commentary, establishing Kay's signature technique of moving between individual lives and the vast currents of history that shape them.

5. **Plant Seeds of Convergence** — Introduce or reference elements (characters, places, tensions) that will connect the parallel storylines, suggesting the eventual intersection of Lin Shan's world with Ren Daiyan's martial path.
