# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *The Years of Rice and Salt*

## "Awake to Emptiness" - Key Structural Points

1. **Establish the Alternate History Premise** - Bold, the Mongol soldier, must discover the devastated, depopulated European villages (plague having killed 99% rather than 33%), creating the foundational "what if" that drives the entire novel.

2. **Introduce the Reincarnation Framework** - Establish Bold and Psin as the first iteration of the "jati" (soul group) whose first-letter names (B and K/P characters) will recur across centuries, signaling the novel's unique narrative structure.

3. **Create Visceral Horror/Wonder** - The emptiness of Europe must land emotionally—the eerie silence, unburied bodies, abandoned wealth—to make readers feel the magnitude of this historical divergence.

4. **Plant Philosophical Seeds** - Through Bold's Buddhist perspective and Psin's pragmatic observations, introduce the novel's meditation on history, fate, and whether civilizations rise by destiny or accident.

5. **End with Transition/Death** - Bold's chapter must conclude in a way that propels the soul-journey forward (his capture/enslavement and eventual death), establishing the bardo interludes and the pattern of rebirth that structures the entire 700-year narrative.
