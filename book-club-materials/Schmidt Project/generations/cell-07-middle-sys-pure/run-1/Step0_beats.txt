# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *The Years of Rice and Salt*

## "Awake to Emptiness" - First Chapter To-Do List

1. **Establish the Alternate History Premise** — Show Bold, a Mongol soldier, discovering abandoned European villages decimated by the Black Death (which killed 99% rather than ~30% of Europe's population), immediately signaling this is not our timeline.

2. **Introduce the Reincarnation Framework** — Present Bold's death and transition to the bardo (Buddhist intermediate state), establishing the novel's central conceit that the same souls will cycle through history together.

3. **Establish the "Jati" (Soul Group)** — Introduce key recurring souls (identifiable by names beginning with the same letter: B, K, I, S) who will reappear across centuries, creating continuity through the 700-year narrative.

4. **Create Philosophical/Spiritual Grounding** — Use the bardo sequences to establish the Buddhist and Islamic philosophical lenses through which history will be examined, setting up the novel's meditation on karma, progress, and meaning.

5. **Launch the Alternate Historical Trajectory** — End with souls being reborn into the newly emptied world, propelling readers into a history where Islam, China, and India will dominate in Europe's absence.
