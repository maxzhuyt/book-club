# Structural Beats for Dutch-Shogunate Negotiation Scene (1880)

## Original Scene To-Do List

1. **Establish the Confined Setting and Power Imbalance** — Show the Dutch merchant operating within Dejima's strict physical and legal boundaries, emphasizing how decades of continued isolation have preserved (or intensified) the shogunate's control over foreign trade and movement, contrasting with how open Japan became in our timeline by 1880.

2. **Introduce the Stakes Through a Specific Request** — Give the merchant a concrete goal (expanded trading rights, new goods approval, access beyond Dejima, or permission for telegraph/steamship technology) that tests the boundaries of the closed system and forces both characters to reveal their positions.

3. **Dramatize the Cultural and Political Tension** — Show the shogunate official navigating between personal pragmatism and bakufu orthodoxy, perhaps hinting at internal debates about whether isolation remains sustainable as Western powers grow stronger elsewhere in Asia.

4. **Deploy Period-Specific Worldbuilding Details** — Weave in how this alternate 1880 differs: no Meiji Restoration, no industrialization, continued rangaku (Dutch learning) as Japan's only Western window, and the official's awareness of colonial pressures on China and Korea.

5. **End on Ambiguous or Delayed Resolution** — Close with neither full success nor outright failure, leaving tension about whether the system can hold and seeding questions about coming pressures that will drive subsequent scenes.
