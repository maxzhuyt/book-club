# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *The Years of Rice and Salt*

## "Awake to Emptiness" - First Chapter To-Do List

1. **Establish the Alternate History Premise** - Open with Bold, a Mongol soldier, discovering abandoned European villages decimated by plague, immediately signaling that the Black Death has killed nearly all of Europe (not just one-third as in our history).

2. **Introduce the Reincarnation Framework** - Present Bold's death and transition to the bardo (Buddhist intermediate state), establishing the novel's central device where the same souls will be reborn across centuries, identifiable by names beginning with the same letter.

3. **Create the "Jati" (Soul Group) Dynamic** - Introduce the core relationship between Bold (B-soul) and the enslaved boy Kyu/later Kheim (K-soul), whose intertwined fates will echo through all subsequent incarnations.

4. **Ground the Mystical in the Visceral** - Balance the spiritual/philosophical elements with stark physical reality—the horror of corpse-filled villages, the texture of survival, the sensory details of 14th-century Mongol life.

5. **Plant Seeds of Historical Divergence** - End with circumstances that propel characters eastward/toward the Islamic and Chinese worlds, setting the stage for the novel's exploration of how history unfolds without European Christendom as a major power.
