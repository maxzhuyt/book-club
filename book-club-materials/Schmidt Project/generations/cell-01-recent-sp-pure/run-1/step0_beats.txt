# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of "Fatherland" by Robert Harris

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Alternate History World** - Introduce the reader to 1964 Nazi Germany where the Reich won WWII, using subtle environmental details (Berlin architecture, propaganda, daily life) to signal this is not our timeline without heavy exposition.

2. **Introduce Xavier March as the Protagonist** - Present the Kripo detective as a morally conflicted man within the system—competent, observant, but quietly disillusioned with the regime, establishing his role as an unlikely investigator.

3. **The Inciting Incident: Discovery of the Body** - March is called to investigate a corpse found in a lake (later identified as a former Nazi official), creating the central mystery that will drive the entire narrative.

4. **Create Immediate Suspense and Unease** - Layer in atmospheric tension through the cold April morning, the grim setting, and hints that this case may be more significant than an ordinary death—something feels wrong from the start.

5. **Signal the Stakes and Opposition** - Introduce early indicators that powerful forces (the SS/Gestapo) may have interest in this case, foreshadowing the danger March will face as he pursues the truth.
