# Structural Beats for Original Scene: "The Directive"
## Berlin, December 1942 - Heydrich's Shadow

### 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Investigator's Routine Disrupted** - Open with the protagonist engaged in mundane police work (paperwork, a minor case, the gray monotony of wartime Berlin) when an unexpected summons arrives bearing Heydrich's office seal, immediately signaling that ordinary life has ended.

2. **Build Dread Through the Journey** - Use the physical passage to Prinz-Albrecht-Straße (or wherever Heydrich's power now centers) to create escalating tension—checkpoints, SS guards, the architecture of intimidation—while revealing the investigator's internal calculus of survival and compromise.

3. **The Intermediary Delivers the Directive** - Rather than meeting Heydrich directly, the investigator receives instructions from a cold functionary who speaks with borrowed authority, making the directive feel both impersonal and inescapable while leaving Heydrich as an unseen menace.

4. **Reveal the Impossible Assignment** - The directive itself must contain inherent contradiction or moral trap—perhaps investigating a death that powerful figures need buried, or locating someone who should not be found—forcing the investigator to recognize there is no safe path forward.

5. **End on a Choice Without Good Options** - Close the scene with the investigator departing into the December cold, now carrying dangerous knowledge, establishing their central dilemma: obey and become complicit, or resist and face annihilation.
