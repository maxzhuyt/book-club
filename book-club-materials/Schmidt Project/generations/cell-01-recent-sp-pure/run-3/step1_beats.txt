# Structural Beats for Original Scene: The Directive from Heydrich's Office

## Berlin, December 1942

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Investigator's Ordinary World** — Open with the protagonist engaged in routine police work amid wartime Berlin's winter atmosphere, grounding the reader in their professional competence and existing caseload before the disruption arrives.

2. **The Summons Arrives with Institutional Weight** — A message from the Reichsführer-SS's office interrupts normal operations, immediately creating tension through colleagues' reactions, the messenger's demeanor, and the protagonist's internal alarm at being noticed by such heights of power.

3. **The Directive Delivered with Calculated Ambiguity** — A subordinate official (not Heydrich himself) conveys orders that are deliberately incomplete or contradictory, forcing the investigator to read between lines while understanding that asking clarifying questions carries its own dangers.

4. **Reveal the Case's Uncomfortable Dimensions** — Through what is said and conspicuously unsaid, establish that this assignment involves either a prominent figure, a sensitive location, or evidence that implicates the regime itself, making the investigator's position immediately precarious.

5. **Close with Compromised Agency** — End the scene with the investigator accepting the directive while privately recognizing they have entered a labyrinth where success and failure may both prove fatal, establishing the thriller's central tension between duty and survival.
