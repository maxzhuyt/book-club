# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *Fatherland* by Robert Harris

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Alternate History World** — Introduce the reader to 1964 Berlin in a timeline where Nazi Germany won WWII, using atmospheric details (architecture, symbols, routine behaviors) to ground the dystopian setting without overwhelming exposition.

2. **Introduce Xavier March as Protagonist** — Present the Kripo detective through his morning routine and professional demeanor, establishing him as a cynical, observant investigator who exists uneasily within the system.

3. **The Inciting Incident: Discovery of the Body** — March is called to investigate a corpse found in a lake (later identified as a former Nazi official), setting the central mystery in motion and creating immediate narrative tension.

4. **Seed Institutional Conflict** — Hint at the friction between the Kripo (criminal police) and the Gestapo/SS, foreshadowing the dangerous political dimensions of the case that will complicate March's investigation.

5. **Create Atmospheric Dread and Irony** — Layer the chapter with details about the upcoming celebration of Hitler's 75th birthday and the "normality" of fascist daily life, establishing the thriller's tone of menace beneath mundane surfaces.
