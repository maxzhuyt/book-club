# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *Fatherland* by Robert Harris

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Alternate History World** — Immediately immerse the reader in a 1964 Nazi Germany that won WWII, using environmental details (Berlin architecture, Hitler's 75th birthday preparations) to signal this is not our timeline without heavy exposition.

2. **Introduce Xavier March as a Flawed Protagonist** — Present the Kripo detective as a skilled but isolated figure, establishing his divorced status, estranged son, and subtle alienation from the regime to create reader sympathy and hint at his capacity for dissent.

3. **The Inciting Incident: Discovery of the Body** — Open with the discovery of an unidentified corpse in the Havel lake, creating immediate mystery and giving March a case that will drive the entire narrative.

4. **Seed Institutional Tension** — Establish the jurisdictional friction between Kripo (criminal police) and the Gestapo/SS, foreshadowing that this case touches something the regime wants buried and that March will face internal enemies.

5. **Create Atmospheric Dread** — Layer in the surveillance state's omnipresence through small details (informants, careful speech, party loyalty questions) to establish the thriller's stakes—investigation in this world means personal danger.
