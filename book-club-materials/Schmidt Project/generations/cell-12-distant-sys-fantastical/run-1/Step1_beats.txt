# Structural Beats for Original Scene: Roman Senate Debates an Oracle's Instruction

## To-Do List for Scene Structure

1. **Establish the Oracle's Pronouncement as Inciting Conflict** — Open with the specific divine instruction already delivered (perhaps from Delphi or the Sibylline Books), making clear its demands are politically inconvenient or factionally divisive, forcing senators to navigate between piety and self-interest.

2. **Ground the Divine as Bureaucratic Reality** — Show through casual references and procedural details that gods intervening is routine but still complicated—augurs officially present, protocols for recording divine communications, perhaps mention of recent consequences when another city ignored similar guidance.

3. **Create Factional Tension Through Interpretation** — Divide the Senate between those arguing for literal compliance, those seeking favorable reinterpretation, and cynics questioning the oracle's authenticity, using this debate to reveal character motivations and the era's political fractures (optimates vs. populares).

4. **Introduce Stakes Through Historical Texture** — Anchor the fictional scenario in 60 BCE's actual instability—the rising influence of Pompey, Caesar, and Crassus—suggesting the oracle's instruction could shift this delicate balance or threaten specific senators' ambitions.

5. **End on Unresolved Tension or Ominous Deferral** — Close without clean resolution, perhaps with a procedural delay, a senator's private scheming, or a subtle divine sign witnessed by few, maintaining narrative momentum while suggesting consequences for Rome's attempted evasion.
