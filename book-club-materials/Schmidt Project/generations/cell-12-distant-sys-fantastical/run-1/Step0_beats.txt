# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *Circe* by Madeline Miller

## To-Do List for First Chapter Structure

1. **Establish the Divine World & Hierarchy** — Introduce Circe's birth to Helios (sun god) and the nymph Perse, situating the reader within the Titan/Olympian power structure and the politics of the gods' realm.

2. **Define Circe as an Outsider** — Establish her "otherness" through her strange voice (compared to mortal tones), unremarkable appearance, and the disdain/dismissal she receives from her divine family, creating immediate sympathy and conflict.

3. **Introduce the Theme of Power vs. Powerlessness** — Show the casual cruelty of the gods (particularly through Helios's indifference and the punishment of Prometheus) to establish what Circe exists within and will eventually push against.

4. **Plant the Prometheus Encounter** — Include Circe's pivotal moment of compassion when she brings water to the tortured Prometheus, marking her first act of defiance and foreshadowing her capacity for empathy that separates her from other gods.

5. **Create Narrative Voice & Tone** — Establish Circe's retrospective, self-aware first-person narration that balances mythic grandeur with intimate, almost modern emotional accessibility, setting up the novel's distinctive storytelling approach.
