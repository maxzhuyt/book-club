# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *Circe* by Madeline Miller

## To-Do List for First Chapter Structure

1. **Establish the narrator's marginal position** — Introduce Circe as an outsider within her own divine family, emphasizing her "thin" voice and mortal-like qualities that set her apart from the Titan gods.

2. **Ground the reader in mythological world-building** — Orient the audience in the palace of Helios beneath the sea, establishing the rules of this divine realm (immortality, power hierarchies, the gods' indifference to mortals).

3. **Introduce the central family dynamics and conflict** — Present Helios's dismissiveness, Perse's cruelty, and the siblings who overshadow Circe, seeding the emotional wounds that will drive her character arc.

4. **Plant Prometheus as a thematic catalyst** — Use the bound Prometheus scene as a pivotal moment where Circe shows compassion (offering him nectar), revealing her divergent nature and foreshadowing her affinity for outcasts and mortals.

5. **Create forward momentum through prophecy/warning** — End with tension about Circe's difference being noticed or questioned, hinting that her unconventional qualities will eventually lead to consequences and exile.

---

These beats establish voice, world, character wound, and thematic seeds while creating narrative pull.
