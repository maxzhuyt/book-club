# Structural Beats for Chapter One of *Circe* by Madeline Miller

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Divine World's Hierarchy and Cruelty** — Open in the halls of Helios, introducing the rigid power structures of the Titans and Olympians, showing how might and beauty determine worth among immortals.

2. **Introduce Circe as an Outsider/Misfit** — Reveal her "inferior" qualities: her strange voice, unremarkable appearance, and lack of obvious power, positioning her as fundamentally different from her divine family.

3. **Show Parental Rejection and Isolation** — Demonstrate her father Helios's indifference and her mother Perse's contempt, establishing the emotional neglect that shapes Circe's character and motivations.

4. **Plant the Seed of Circe's Unique Perspective** — Include her encounter with Prometheus (being punished), where she alone shows him compassion, hinting at her capacity for empathy that separates her from other gods.

5. **Foreshadow Her Transformation/Journey** — End with details that suggest her latent potential and hint at the witchcraft/power that will eventually define her, creating narrative tension about who she might become.

---

*These beats establish voice, world, character wound, and thematic concerns (power, isolation, transformation) that drive the entire novel.*
