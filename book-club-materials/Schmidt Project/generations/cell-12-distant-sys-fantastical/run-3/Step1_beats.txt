# Structural Beats for Original Scene: Roman Senate Debates Divine Oracle

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Political Stakes Before Divine Intervention** — Open mid-session with senators already divided over a pressing secular matter (military campaign, territorial dispute, resource allocation), grounding the scene in recognizable Roman political tension and factional maneuvering.

2. **Introduce the Oracle's Instruction as Disruption** — Have a priest or official messenger formally present the specific divine mandate, which directly contradicts or complicates the preferred course of at least one powerful faction, forcing the collision between divine authority and political self-interest.

3. **Stage the Interpretive Battle** — Show senators arguing not whether to obey but how to interpret the oracle's words, revealing character through rhetorical strategies: the literalist, the convenient reinterpreter, the skeptic who dares not voice full doubt, the true believer.

4. **Demonstrate the Consequences of Divine Reality** — Include concrete reference or evidence that disobedience carries genuine supernatural risk (a previous defiance's catastrophic outcome, an omen occurring during debate, a senator's personal testimony of witnessed divine action), raising stakes beyond mere piety.

5. **Force a Compromised Resolution** — End with a decision that attempts to satisfy both divine mandate and political necessity, leaving key characters privately uncertain or troubled, establishing tensions that ripple forward into subsequent narrative.
