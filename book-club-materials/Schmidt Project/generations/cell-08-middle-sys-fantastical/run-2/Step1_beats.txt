# Structural Beats for Original Scene: "The Mammoth Question"

**London, 1872 — A Meeting of the Imperial Society for Megafaunal Studies**

---

## 5 Key Structural Beats

1. **Establish Institutional Stakes and Status Quo** — Open with the Society gathered in their wood-paneled chambers, revealing through dialogue and detail that megafauna collection has become fiercely competitive among European powers, and that Britain's Siberian specimens are embarrassingly outdated compared to the Prussian and French collections.

2. **Introduce the Proposal and Its Champion** — Have an ambitious naturalist (perhaps younger, perhaps with something to prove) present the expedition plan to the Yeniseisk mammoth grounds, complete with maps, cost estimates, and passionate arguments about scientific prestige—while facing skepticism from established members.

3. **Surface the Central Conflict Through Debate** — Allow opposing voices to raise legitimate obstacles: Russian diplomatic tensions, the ethical controversy over live capture versus taxidermy, the disastrous 1868 ground sloth expedition, and the question of whether the declining mammoth herds can sustain further collection pressure.

4. **Complicate with New Information** — Introduce a disrupting element mid-meeting: perhaps a letter arrives reporting that a German expedition has already departed, or someone reveals classified intelligence about mammoth population collapse, forcing the Society to reconsider their timeline and methods.

5. **End on a Decision That Opens Future Tension** — Conclude with a vote or pronouncement that nominally resolves the immediate question but creates narrative momentum: the expedition is approved with conditions, or rejected but privately funded, leaving personal and institutional conflicts unresolved.
