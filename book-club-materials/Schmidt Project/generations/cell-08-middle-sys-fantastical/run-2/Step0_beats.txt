# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *Jonathan Strange & Mr Norrell*

**"The Library at Hurtfew"** (Note: The novel actually opens with a different chapter - "The Friends of English Magic")

---

## 5 Key Structural Beats for Chapter 1: "The Friends of English Magic"

1. **Establish the World's Central Tension** — Introduce the York Society of Magicians as "theoretical" magicians who only study magic but cannot perform it, immediately establishing that magic in England has become extinct in practice.

2. **Introduce the Inciting Disruption** — Bring Segundus's provocative question ("Why is no magic done in England?") that disturbs the Society's comfortable stagnation and sets the plot in motion.

3. **Plant the Mystery of Mr Norrell** — Reference the reclusive Mr Norrell of Hurtfew Abbey as someone who claims to be a *practical* magician, creating intrigue and the chapter's driving question.

4. **Create the Demonstration/Proof Scene** — Structure toward the climactic moment when Norrell animates the stone statues in York Cathedral, providing undeniable proof that magic has returned to England.

5. **Establish the Narrative Voice** — Firmly ground the mock-scholarly, footnote-laden, Austenian narrator who treats magic as historical fact, setting the tonal foundation for the entire novel.
