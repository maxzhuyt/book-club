# Structural Beats: Chapter 1 of *Jonathan Strange & Mr Norrell*

**"The Library at Hurtfew"** (Note: Actually Chapter 1 is "The Friends of English Magic")

1. **Establish the World's Central Question** — Introduce the York Society of magicians and their debate over whether magic can be *practiced* again in England, setting up the novel's core tension between theoretical and practical magic.

2. **Create the Inciting Disruption** — Mr. Segundus asks the forbidden question ("Why is no magic done in England?"), breaking social decorum and catalyzing the plot's movement toward finding a *real* magician.

3. **Introduce the Mysterious Antagonist/Catalyst** — Plant Mr. Norrell as an unseen but powerful presence through reputation alone; the Society learns he claims to be a *practicing* magician, creating intrigue and skepticism.

4. **Establish Tone and Narrative Voice** — Ground the reader in the novel's distinctive mock-scholarly, Austenian narrative style through footnotes, dry wit, and period-appropriate social observation.

5. **Set Up the First Movement's Goal** — End with the decision to visit Norrell at Hurtfew Abbey, giving the narrative clear forward momentum and promising the eventual revelation of whether "real" magic exists.
