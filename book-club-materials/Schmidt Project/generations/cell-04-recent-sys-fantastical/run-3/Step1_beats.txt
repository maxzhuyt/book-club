# Key Structural Beats for the Munitions Inspector Scene

1. **Establish the Hybrid Production Environment** - Open with sensory details that blend industrial factory elements (furnaces, conveyor belts, metal shavings) with magical infrastructure (ritual circles embedded in floors, glyphs chalked on machinery, ambient supernatural residue), normalizing this fusion as routine workplace reality.

2. **Introduce the Inspector's Dual Expertise** - Demonstrate through action and observation that the protagonist must evaluate both metallurgical quality and ritual integrity; show their competence through specific technical assessments that reveal character while teaching readers the rules of this world.

3. **Discover a Discrepancy or Anomaly** - The inspection uncovers something wrong with the night shift's output—whether a metallurgical flaw, a ritual shortcut, sabotage, or an unexplained enhancement—creating tension and raising questions that demand investigation or decision.

4. **Reveal Wartime Pressures Through Human Cost** - Use interactions with workers, production quotas, or physical evidence to show how the war's demands strain both the mundane workforce and the magical practitioners, grounding the fantastical elements in human exhaustion, fear, or moral compromise.

5. **Force a Choice or Judgment** - End the scene with the inspector facing a consequential decision about the discrepancy: report it, ignore it, investigate further, or confront someone—establishing their values and setting narrative momentum into motion.
