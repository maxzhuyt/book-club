# Key Structural Beats for Original Scene: Birmingham Munitions Inspection

## Premise Context
Magic returned in 1914; by 1942 weapons production blends metallurgy and ritual

---

1. **Establish the Hybrid Production Environment** - Ground the reader in the physical space where industrial machinery and ritual implements coexist. Show the tangible evidence of both processes—furnace glow alongside chalk circles, conveyor belts passing through consecrated stations, workers wearing both safety goggles and warding amulets.

2. **Introduce the Inspector's Dual Expertise** - Reveal through action and observation that competent inspection now requires evaluating both metallurgical quality and ritual integrity. The inspector should demonstrate fluency in checking tolerances AND detecting flawed enchantments, establishing this hybrid skillset as normal workplace competence.

3. **Surface the Tension of Wartime Production Pressure** - Show the competing demands of quantity versus quality when shortcuts in either domain could prove catastrophic. Night shift output numbers tell a story—perhaps impressive yields but something feels wrong, or disappointing numbers that reflect necessary caution.

4. **Discover a Specific Problem Requiring Decision** - The inspector finds something concerning in the night's output that demands judgment. This creates immediate stakes and reveals character through how they weigh practical, ethical, and bureaucratic considerations.

5. **Hint at the Larger War Context** - Without heavy exposition, suggest what these munitions are for and why they matter. A shipping manifest destination, a rumor about the front, or simply the exhausted faces of workers conveys the weight pressing down on this single inspection.
