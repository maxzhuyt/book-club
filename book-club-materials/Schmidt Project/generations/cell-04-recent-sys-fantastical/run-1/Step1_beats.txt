# Key Structural Beats for Original Munitions Inspection Scene

1. **Establish the Dual Nature of Production Through Routine** - Show the inspector moving through their checklist in a way that reveals both conventional metallurgical standards (tolerances, alloy composition, cooling rates) and ritualistic requirements (proper inscriptions, binding integrity, resonance checks) as equally mundane aspects of quality control.

2. **Reveal Character Through Professional Concerns** - Use the inspector's internal observations and judgments about the night shift's work to establish their expertise, personality, and priorities. What flaws do they find unacceptable? What shortcuts do they notice? This grounds the fantastical premise in workaday realism.

3. **Introduce Tension Through a Specific Anomaly** - During the review, the inspector discovers something wrong with a batch—perhaps a ritual component was performed incorrectly, materials were substituted, or something more mysterious occurred. This problem should carry weight given wartime production pressures.

4. **Convey Worldbuilding Through Consequence** - Show what happens when munitions fail their inspection. The stakes of improper ritual work should feel tangible—whether that means dangerous instability, wasted resources under rationing, or disciplinary implications for workers.

5. **End on a Decision Point or Unresolved Question** - Leave the inspector facing a choice or confronting something that extends beyond routine inspection, propelling narrative momentum beyond this single scene.
