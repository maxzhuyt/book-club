# Structural Beats for the Study Scene

**1. Establish the Weight of Ordinary Time**
Open with the minister engaged in the mundane labor of sermon-writing—crossing out phrases, consulting scripture, pausing over word choices—grounding his extraordinary experience in the texture of continued pastoral duty and the passage of unremarkable months.

**2. Define the Theologian's Stance Through Physical Detail**
Reveal the visitor's intellectual position not through stated argument but through telling behaviors—how he handles his notes, where his eyes settle, the quality of his silences—establishing whether he arrives as skeptic, believer, or something more complicated seeking ammunition.

**3. Surface the Minister's Private Doubt or Certainty**
Through the sermon draft itself, show the minister wrestling with how to translate his experience into language fit for Sunday consumption—what he includes, what he omits, and what this editorial process reveals about his own evolving relationship to what he witnessed.

**4. Let Theological Tension Emerge Through Craft Discussion**
Allow disagreement to surface obliquely through conversation about rhetoric, word choice, or scriptural interpretation rather than direct confrontation—two professionals discussing the *how* while the *what* of the afterlife testimony looms unspoken.

**5. Close on an Unresolved Exchange**
End the scene with a question asked or implication left hanging—something that shifts the power dynamic or reveals unexpected common ground—propelling narrative momentum without artificial resolution.
