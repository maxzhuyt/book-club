# Structural Beats for Chapter One of *The Difference Engine*

**1. Establish Sybil Gerard's Compromised Position**
Introduce the protagonist as a fallen woman/former courtesan navigating London's demimonde, immediately grounding the reader in the novel's class-conscious alternate Victorian society.

**2. World-Building Through Texture, Not Exposition**
Layer in details of the steam-powered, Babbage-engine-driven Britain organically—through Sybil's observations, social interactions, and environment—establishing the alternate 1855 without info-dumps.

**3. Introduce the Political Undercurrent**
Reference Sybil's connection to her executed Luddite father (Walter Gerard), establishing the tension between Industrial Radical rule and those crushed by technological "progress."

**4. The Mick Radley Entanglement**
Develop Sybil's relationship with the adventurer/gambler Mick Radley, creating immediate narrative momentum through their schemes while hinting at larger conspiracies involving powerful figures.

**5. Plant the MacGuffin/Mystery Element**
Seed the chapter with references to valuable information, punch cards, or political secrets that will drive the larger plot—connecting Sybil's personal survival story to the novel's broader technological-political thriller.

---
*These beats establish Gibson & Sterling's signature style: character-driven noir atmosphere layered over meticulously researched speculative world-building.*
