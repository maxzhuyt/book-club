# Structural Beats for the Study Scene

**1. Establish the Minister's Changed Relationship with Ordinary Tasks**
Show him struggling with sermon-writing that once came naturally—the gap between what he experienced and what language can convey, creating tension between his pastoral duty and his private knowledge.

**2. The Theologian's Dual Purpose**
Reveal through dialogue and body language whether the visitor comes as ally or skeptic, colleague or inquisitor—his notes suggesting systematic evaluation rather than casual conversation, introducing institutional stakes.

**3. A Concrete Detail from the Testimony Under Examination**
Have the theologian press on one specific claim from the minister's account, forcing the minister to either defend, qualify, or admit uncertainty—grounding the metaphysical in a debatable particular.

**4. The Weight of the Year Between**
Convey through environmental details or interior reflection how twelve months of scrutiny, correspondence, and unwanted fame have worn on the minister—the study itself perhaps showing evidence of this changed life.

**5. An Unresolved Question Left Hanging**
End the scene with either the theologian or minister raising something that cannot be answered in this room—a challenge to faith, a medical counterargument, a personal cost—that propels the narrative forward without neat resolution.

---
*These beats balance intellectual discourse with character interiority, keeping theological abstraction grounded in human stakes.*
