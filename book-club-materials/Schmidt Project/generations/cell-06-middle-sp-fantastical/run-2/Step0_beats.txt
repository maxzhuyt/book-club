# Structural Beats for Chapter One of *The Difference Engine*

**1. Establish Sybil Gerard's Status and Vulnerability**
Introduce the protagonist as a "fallen woman" and daughter of a disgraced Luddite leader, immediately grounding her precarious social position in this alternate Victorian world.

**2. World-Building Through Texture**
Embed the alternate history naturally—Charles Babbage's Engines have transformed Britain into a technocratic "Rad" (Radical) governed society, revealed through casual details rather than exposition dumps.

**3. The Mick Radley Relationship**
Establish Sybil's dependency on the low-level criminal/tout Mick, creating immediate tension about her survival strategies and moral compromises in London's underworld.

**4. Introduction of the "Modus" MacGuffin**
Plant the mysterious punch cards (the "modus") that will drive much of the novel's intrigue, even if their significance isn't yet clear—setting up the information-as-power theme.

**5. Encounter with Dandy Mick's Political Connections**
Sybil's brush with figures connected to larger political machinations (including the suggestion of powerful patrons) opens the door to the conspiracy plot while keeping her as an unwitting participant at society's margins.

---
*These beats establish the noir-inflected tone and class-conscious steampunk world that defines the novel.*
