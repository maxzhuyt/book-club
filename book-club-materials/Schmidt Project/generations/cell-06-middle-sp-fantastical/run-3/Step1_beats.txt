# Structural Beats for the Study Scene

**1. Establish the Minister's Changed Relationship with Routine**
Open with the physical act of sermon-writing—pen, paper, familiar desk—while revealing through small details how this once-automatic pastoral duty now feels alien or weighted differently after his experience.

**2. The Theologian's Agenda Beneath Courtesy**
Through dialogue and body language, make clear the visitor has traveled with specific intent—whether to validate, debunk, co-opt, or warn—creating intellectual tension that simmers beneath collegial pleasantries.

**3. The Sermon Draft as Battlefield**
Use the emerging text itself as contested ground; what the minister chooses to include, omit, or hedge reveals his internal struggle between testimony and doubt, pastoral responsibility and personal truth.

**4. A Moment of Involuntary Memory**
Interrupt the theological sparring with a sensory trigger—afternoon light, a word, the scratch of pen—that pulls the minister briefly back toward his experience, showing the reader its lingering phenomenological weight without over-describing it.

**5. An Unresolved Question Left Hanging**
End with the theologian posing a challenge or the minister writing a line he immediately crosses out, leaving the scene's central tension—what can be responsibly said about what he witnessed—deliberately unresolved and propulsive.

---
*These beats balance intellectual debate, character interiority, and the premise's epistemological uncertainty.*
