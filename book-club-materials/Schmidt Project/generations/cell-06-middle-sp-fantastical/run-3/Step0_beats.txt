# Structural Beats for Chapter One of *The Difference Engine*

**1. Establish Sybil Gerard's Compromised Position**
Introduce the protagonist as a "fallen woman" and former courtesan, immediately grounding the reader in her precarious social status and survival instincts in this alternate Victorian world.

**2. World-Building Through Immersive Detail**
Weave in the divergent history—Babbage's mechanical computers succeeded, the Industrial Radical Party rules Britain—through environmental description and casual references rather than exposition dumps.

**3. The Mick Radley Relationship & Underworld Connection**
Establish Sybil's dependence on and entanglement with the charismatic "flash" man Mick, revealing the criminal/political demimonde that operates beneath respectable society.

**4. Introduce the MacGuffin: The Mysterious Punch Cards**
Plant the central mystery object—a set of Engine cards of unknown but clearly dangerous significance—that will drive the plot and connect the narrative's multiple threads.

**5. Create Atmospheric Tension & Forward Momentum**
End the chapter with Sybil in motion toward uncertain danger, establishing the noir-inflected tone and sense that larger political machinations are already in play beyond her understanding.

---
*These beats balance character introduction, steampunk world-establishment, and plot propulsion essential for launching the mosaic narrative structure.*
