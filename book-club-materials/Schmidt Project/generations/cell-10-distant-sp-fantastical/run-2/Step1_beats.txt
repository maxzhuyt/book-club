# Structural Beats for the Reliquary Court Evaluation Scene

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the weight of institutional procedure** - Open with the formal convening of the court, emphasizing the careful choreography of ecclesiastical authority: the arrangement of bishops and canons, the presence of royal notaries, the invocation of proper protocols. Ground readers in the understanding that this institution exists precisely because miracles are usually *not* verified—making what follows extraordinary.

2. **Introduce competing witness perspectives** - Present two or three cleric-witnesses in the moments before testimony, each processing the event through different lenses: one shaken to his foundations in genuine faith, one searching for rational explanations he cannot find, one already calculating political implications. Their internal preparations reveal the event's destabilizing power.

3. **Create tension through the burden of testimony** - Show the first witness called forward, capturing the terrible weight of speaking words that cannot be unsaid. His hesitation, his awareness that his testimony will be copied and sent to Rome, his struggle to render the ineffable into notarial language—all underscore that history pivots on this moment.

4. **Dramatize the failure of familiar frameworks** - Have the questioning expose how existing categories for evaluating relics collapse when confronted with something undeniable. Standard skeptical inquiries feel hollow; the usual escape routes of fraud or misperception close one by one.

5. **Close on irreversible momentum** - End with the court acknowledging it must proceed toward authentication, a decision carrying profound unspoken implications. The machinery of the Church begins turning toward a conclusion that will reshape Christendom.
