# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of *Doomsday Book*

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the dual-timeline premise** - Introduce Kivrin Engle preparing for her time-travel journey to medieval England (1320) while grounding readers in the near-future Oxford setting, making clear the stakes of this unprecedented solo expedition to a dangerous era.

2. **Create immediate tension through opposition** - Show Mr. Dunworthy's deep reservations and protective concern about the drop, establishing him as the worried mentor figure whose unease foreshadows coming disasters and creates dramatic irony.

3. **Plant seeds of systemic failure** - Introduce subtle signs that things are not quite right—rushed preparations, Gilchrist's incompetence/arrogance, the tech Badri's concerning behavior—laying groundwork for the catastrophic errors to come.

4. **Execute the point of no return** - Complete Kivrin's departure through the net, physically separating her from the present-day characters and locking in the narrative's dual-strand structure.

5. **End on ominous uncertainty** - Close with Badri's collapse and cryptic warning that something is wrong with the fix/coordinates, creating an immediate cliffhanger that propels readers forward while establishing the novel's central mystery and sense of dread.
