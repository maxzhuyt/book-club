# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of "Doomsday Book"

1. **Establish the Time-Travel Premise & Stakes** - Introduce Kivrin's imminent departure to medieval England (1320), the Oxford history department's time-travel technology, and the experimental/risky nature of this particular drop into a "ten" rated dangerous period.

2. **Create Immediate Conflict Through Opposition** - Show Mr. Dunworthy's deep concern and active resistance to the drop proceeding, establishing his protective relationship with Kivrin and his distrust of the acting department head Gilchrist, who has approved the mission prematurely.

3. **Build Tension Through Illness/Warning Signs** - Introduce Badri (the tech) feeling unwell at the critical moment of the drop, planting the seed of something wrong that will escalate into the novel's dual-timeline crisis.

4. **Execute the Point of No Return** - Complete Kivrin's departure to the past despite the mounting concerns, creating an irreversible action that locks both timelines into their trajectories and separates the protagonists.

5. **Establish Dual-World Structure** - Ground the reader in the 2054 Oxford setting with its Christmas preparations, bureaucratic university politics, and technological normalcy, creating contrast with the medieval world Kivrin enters.
