# Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of "Doomsday Book"

1. **Establish the Time Travel Premise & Stakes** - Introduce Kivrin's imminent departure to medieval England (1320) and the experimental nature of this first historian drop to such a dangerous period, creating immediate tension about what could go wrong.

2. **Create Institutional Conflict** - Show the clash between Mr. Dunworthy's protective concerns about Kivrin's safety and the ambitious Mediaeval Department head Gilchrist, who is pushing the drop forward despite inadequate preparation—establishing the flawed decision-making that will have consequences.

3. **Plant Seeds of Technical Malfunction** - Introduce Badri, the tech running the net, who appears increasingly unwell during the drop procedure, foreshadowing both the plague storyline and the possibility that something has gone wrong with the coordinates.

4. **Anchor Emotional Investment** - Develop Dunworthy as the worried mentor/father-figure whose POV we'll follow in 2054 Oxford, making his helplessness as Kivrin departs the emotional core readers will return to throughout the parallel narratives.

5. **Execute the Point of No Return** - Complete the drop itself, sending Kivrin into the past and severing communication, which locks in the dual-timeline structure and creates the central dramatic question: Is she where (and when) she's supposed to be?
