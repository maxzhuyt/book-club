# Structural Beats for the Reliquary Court Evaluation Scene

1. **Establish the Charged Atmosphere of Institutional Crisis** - Open with the physical and emotional texture of the court reconvening: the unusual summer heat, the hushed tension among assembled clergy, the presence of unexpected high-ranking observers who have arrived hastily from Toledo or Rome, signaling that the Church hierarchy already senses this event threatens to upend centuries of careful ambiguity about miracles.

2. **Introduce Competing Witness Perspectives Before Testimony** - Show two or three cleric-witnesses in the moments before they speak, each privately wrestling with what they saw: one absolutely certain and spiritually transformed, one terrified of the implications and searching for natural explanations, one politically calculating how testimony might advance or destroy careers—establishing that even eyewitnesses cannot agree on meaning.

3. **Dramatize the Procedural Stakes Through a Presiding Figure** - Center a bishop or papal legate who must control these proceedings, revealing through his management of protocol his awareness that whatever verdict emerges will ripple outward: if declared authentic, every relic in Christendom must be questioned; if suppressed, those who witnessed cannot simply unknow what they saw.

4. **Surface the Specific Evidentiary Problem** - Through dialogue or internal reflection, articulate what made this miracle undeniable where thousands before were dismissible—perhaps the notarial documentation, the number of witnesses, the nature of the manifestation itself—forcing characters and readers to confront why *this* event breaks the pattern.

5. **End on Institutional Fracture Beginning** - Close the scene with testimony interrupted, postponed, or concluded in a way that reveals the court cannot contain what has happened through normal channels, whether through a witness recanting under pressure, another doubling down despite threats, or the presiding authority recognizing that no verdict he renders will hold.
