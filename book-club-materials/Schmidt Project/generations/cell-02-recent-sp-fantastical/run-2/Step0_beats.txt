# Key Structural Beats for Chapter 1 of "11/22/63"

Here are the 5 most important structural elements that drive the opening chapter:

1. **Establishing the Narrator's Ordinary World** - The chapter introduces Jake Epping as a high school English teacher in small-town Maine, grounding readers in his everyday life, his recent divorce, and his connection to adult education students before anything extraordinary occurs.

2. **The Inciting Incident/Call to Adventure** - Jake's friend Al Templeton, who runs a local diner, summons Jake urgently and reveals a shocking secret: a time portal in his pantry that leads back to 1958. This shatters Jake's normal reality.

3. **The Demonstration of the Impossible** - Al doesn't just tell Jake about time travel—he proves it. This "seeing is believing" moment transforms abstract possibility into concrete reality for both Jake and the reader.

4. **Revealing Al's Mission and Condition** - The chapter establishes dramatic stakes by revealing Al is dying of cancer AND that he's been attempting to prevent the JFK assassination. His failing health creates urgency.

5. **Passing the Torch** - The chapter builds toward Al asking Jake to take over his mission, setting up the central question and moral dilemma that will drive the entire novel: Should Jake go back in time to stop Lee Harvey Oswald?

These beats efficiently move from normalcy to extraordinary possibility to moral challenge.
