# Structural Beats: First Chapter of *11/22/63*

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish the Ordinary World & Protagonist's Stagnation**
   - Introduce Jake Epping as a divorced high school English teacher in Lisbon Falls, Maine, living a routine, somewhat unfulfilling life that makes him receptive to extraordinary change.

2. **Present the Catalyst: Al's Summons**
   - Al Templeton, the diner owner, urgently calls Jake to meet him—appearing mysteriously aged and ill overnight—creating immediate intrigue and disrupting Jake's normal existence.

3. **Reveal the Portal/Time Travel Mechanism**
   - Al shows Jake the "rabbit hole" in the diner's pantry that leads to September 9, 1958, establishing the rules: it's always the same moment, only two minutes pass in the present, and each trip resets everything.

4. **Deliver the Mission/Stakes**
   - Al reveals his failed mission to prevent JFK's assassination, explains why he believes Oswald acted alone, and passes the torch to Jake—establishing the central dramatic question of the novel.

5. **Create Emotional Investment Through Harry Dunning's Story**
   - Weave in the essay about Harry Dunning's family being murdered by his father, providing a smaller-scale "test case" that humanizes the stakes and gives Jake (and readers) an emotional entry point into the larger mission.
