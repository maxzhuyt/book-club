# Structural Beats: The Incomprehensible Broadcast

## 5-Point To-Do List

1. **Establish Routine & Expertise**
   - Show the officer's developed protocol for operating the device after two months of use—his confidence, his systematic approach to logging broadcasts, his belief that he understands what this machine can offer and its strategic value.

2. **Introduce the Anomalous Transmission**
   - The device tunes to an unexpected frequency or date; the broadcast differs qualitatively from previous intercepts—perhaps the language is wrong, the tone unprecedented, or the technical terminology unfamiliar, immediately signaling something outside his framework.

3. **Document the Officer's Attempt to Decode**
   - Show his active effort to make meaning: replaying sections, cross-referencing with known intelligence, asking a subordinate for input, writing fragments down—demonstrating both his competence and the genuine strangeness of what he's hearing.

4. **Plant Specific Details That Will Gain Meaning Later**
   - Embed concrete but currently opaque references—a city name, a date, a number, a phrase—that the reader may recognize or that will pay off in subsequent scenes, creating dramatic irony or foreshadowing.

5. **End on Unresolved Tension**
   - The officer must choose what to do with incomprehensible intelligence: file it, escalate it, dismiss it, or obsess over it privately. His decision reveals character while leaving the mystery open, pulling the narrative forward.
