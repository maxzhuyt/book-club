REVIEW 1 (for "My Autobiography"):
I do love a well-written autobiography. This one is fascinating. CC spent his later years just a few miles down Lake Geneva where I live, so I have a particular kind of connection to him. A few years ago I visited the CC museum, which is located in his villa, and so I could envision his life there as I was reading.CC had, in some ways, a difficult childhood. Certainly he was very poor and had to start working, mainly on stage, at age eight. On the other hand, he had a mother and a brother who he adored and who adored him. It is remarkable that someone who had so little formal education could become so erudite in various respects. When he went to the U.S. for the first time, he was in his teens, and he could barely read. But he had a thirst for knowledge and a drive to excel. Thus he not only wrote, produced and acted in his own movies, he also wrote most of the music. What came across in this book was that he was a totally honest man who always did and said what he most sincerely believed was right. Though he was more of a pacifist that a communist, he was blacklisted by the press and politicians. Certainly his penchant for (very) young women did not help, but when one sees how happy he an Oona (thirty years his junior) were together, who is to judge what is right or wrong in this respect?Especially in light of what is going on in the USA today, his remarks and observations made before and during both world wars are highly relevant.
